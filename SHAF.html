SHAF

After graduating and becoming independent, I started living on my own. Unlike living with family, the biggest problem when living alone is that there is a lot of food waste. Nowadays, as the number of people living alone is increasing, I expected that there would be many people with the same concerns as me. Also, as environmental awareness is rapidly promoted, the movement to solve food waste problem will increase. I have created this application with the intention of solving the problem of one-person households' food waste.

  • Timeline: 1 month and half

  • Client: Personal Project

  • Tools: Figma, Photoshop

  • Role: Research, Wireframe, Design, Prototype

1. Average daily amount of garbage discharged by household

As of 2020, the amount of food waste generated in Seoul is 2,540 tons per day, accounting for 26.3% of the total household waste. Among them, single-person households generate the largest amount of garbage at 207g.

2. Trend of single-person households in Korea

Meanwhile, the number of single-person households is gradually increasing, and the proportion of single-person households in total households based on resident registration exceeded 40% for the first time ever in the third quarter of 2021.

3. Interest in environmental issues

Regarding the overall environment, the number of people who answered that they are “interested (very interested + interested)” reached 74.2% in 2018, and 7 out of 10 Koreans showed high interest in the environment.

Insight

Considering the growing number of one-person households and the ever-increasing environmental awareness, measures to reduce food waste are stronly needed.

A sharing economy system that can handle food waste in smart way for single-person households

1. Check the food around me in real time

◼ Take a look at the refrigerators

Users can search for food ingredients in nearby refrigerators in real time and get a share of the ingredients they want.


◼ Search for a specific food

Users can search for the ingredients of which they are in need and see where the ingredients are located on a map.

2. Sharing food in a way of ensuring freshness and safety

Step 1

Be shared

Step 2

Start the timer


: It keeps food fresh by activating a timer so that users can take food on time.

Step 3

Generate the QR code


: The reliability of the service is increased by granting authority with a QR code so that only authorized users can open the refrigerator.

3. Encourage sharing with a clear reward system

Step 1

Share food

Step 2

Get a reward


: It helps feel a sense of belonging between the pople who practice generosity through the hashtag #Givers, and encourages sharing through a reward system called badges.

4. Send a thank you message after sharing

It allows users to share thanks messages so as not to forget the affection between people.


But if there is a problem with the food, users can report it right away!

When there is a problem with the shared food, users can report it immediately so that other users do not experience a same inconvenience

5. Checking my status for overall services

On the My Page, users can easily check service usage records, such as badges they have collected, sharing records, and thank you messages received. In addition, users can see the foods they saved at a glance, the application suggests foods that can be shared based on previously saved food.

What I've Learned

1. Trust in service is a key factor

Since the service is particularly related to food ingredients, I tried to find a way to give users trust, and this became the most important goal of the project.


2. Process clarity is important

When I first conceived the service, the process of sharing-being shared was not clear, so there were many parts where I myself was confused. By creating a user flow, it was able to clearly redefine the service process, and I tried to devise a process that users could understand as much as possible.


3. The purpose of using the service may be different for each user

Since the service was divided into two types of users, those who share and those who receive sharing, it was important to devise a service flow according to the user's purpose of use. The application was created so that two types of users can achieve their goals without being confused in the same app.
